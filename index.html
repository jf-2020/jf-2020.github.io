About Me

Financial Analyst Turned Math Graduate Student


In a former life, you'd find me wading through any and all SEC filings aplenty. To be buried in the footnotes was a fact of life, and I loved it. At some point though, I realized old school methods weren't the only valid way to generate alpha, and so it was that I stumbled onto mathematical finance. But this new found value mine opened my eyes to a separate universe, the one of Higher Mathematics. And simply put, I fell in love. Quite literally, I felt as if I'd been missing out on this buffet of knowledge, devouring all the math I could in spare time. Not being able to shake this feeling, a new chapter presented itself, for it was then I decided to pursue Mathematics proper, that is, academically.

  Lecture Goals

  • To introduce the notion of confidence intervals via a blend of practical & theoretical approaches
  • To provide a hook or motivation for why we might want to do such a thing using a bird's eye view, namely, the Big Picture of Statistics
  • To give a brief development of the general form any confidence interval might take, followed by one for a population proportion in particular
  • To offer a visualization of what a confidence interval really is and how one might correctly interpret it
  • To demonstrate how to physically compute one with a common software package, that is, Excel
  • To deliver all of this in a written way, as if it were given in a regular classroom setting
